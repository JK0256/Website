Mechanical Engineering - Rube Goldburg Machine (9/8/23-9/15/23)

We used Tinkercad.com to create an initial prototype of a Rube Goldburg machine we'll be making. We need to start the machine with a baseball and end it with a balloon popping. We plan to roll a ball tied to a string to lift a block in front of a hot wheels, then allowing the hot wheels to trigger a pulley that will drop a tungstun cube (courtesy of Duncan) to smash the balloon.


We found some issues with our original design. For example, the original trigger of the final pulley was a ring hooked to a rotating shaft (that would be pushed by the hot wheel), but we found that it would be too difficult to have the shaft rotate through the car hitting it.

We started to put together our physical product, and we decided to have a pulley hanging off of the table, rather than creating two supports for the pulley to stand up on the table. Also, we turned the ramp sideways rather than pointing it away from the pulley.

Our tungstun cube is tied using a string with a ring on the end, and the ring has a rod which is held in place by two supports so that the rod doesn't go toward the cube, but rather so that the rod is yanked sideways as the ball tied to the rod falls to the floor off of the ramp. This was made possible in part because we turned the ramp sideways.

We added another ramp for a hot wheels leading up to the ramp that the ball rolls down so that the hot wheels can hit the ball, and trigger the rest of the Rube Goldburg machine.


So now the process is: hot wheels rolls down the taller ramp, the hot wheels hits the baseball on lower ramp, baseball falls to floor and pulls the rod that its connected to by string, the rod gets pulled out of the ring that is connected to the string that is connected to the tungstun cube, and the tungstun cube falls and pops the balloon.


Thankfully, it worked on our first try!


Computer Engineering - Computer Disection

For this unit, computer engineering, our main project was disecting a computer. Following the directions given on Google Classroom, we identified pieces and cables in the computer that we needed to detach in a certain order, and after a bit of trial and error, we managed to completely dissassemble and then re-assemble the computer! Only thing being, it didn't actually turn on when we were done, though we don't really know if it was supposed to work anyway.


Bioengineering - Hand

We're starting a bioengineering unit now. We saw some pretty cool videos on the uses of bioengineering, such silicone artificial heart and the robot cheetah.

Our main project this time was making a prosthetic hand out of cardboard, rubber bands, and string. It ended up being a bit of a tricky project, because our hand wasn't exactly simple or elegent. We didn't use string but rather the metal wire that Duncan brought, which made it look cool but kind of janky, unfortunately.

The prosthetic hand was supposed to be able to grab a water bottle, and ours did manage, but admittedly with some difficulty. Still, a success is a success!


Aerodynamics - Bottle Rockets

For our aerodynamics unit, we did another classic science project: bottle rockets! This turned out to be quite a bit more intensive than I thought it would be, and there were definitely more details than I there would be. For example: how to attach the parachute, or how to attach the cap, or how to attach the fins. There were many different ways we could do each thing, but through brainstorming and a bit of trial and error, Duncan and I created a pretty neat rocket with a plastic bag parachute inside a bottle nose, diagonally-placed fins, and quite a bit of super glue.


Unfortunately, our rocket didn't work too well. It flew relatively straight and in an arc. The diagonally-placed fins really helped with making the rocket rotate in the air while it flew. However, the nose was way too heavy because of our parachute inside it, and our rocket barely flew through the air for less than a second.


Environmental Engineering - Water Filters

We started with learning about environmental engineering, with a bit of an emphasis on water filteration. The water filteration/treatment system was actually very interesting to see. How do humans come to create such complicated yet efficient systems for so mony aspects of life?

After learning about the modern water filter system, we had our own chance at creating water filters. Using large rocks, small rocks, sand, and cotton balls, we created layers of filtering in a bottle. Duncan and I tried multiple different arrangements, with different bottles, but ultimately, we couldn't get the water filter to produce clear water. Oddly enough, no one else from our class was able to fully filter the dirty water as well.
